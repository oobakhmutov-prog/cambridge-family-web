# PROJECT_BRIEF.md

## Project

Cambridge Family website rebuild

## Product summary

Cambridge Family is an educational space that combines kindergarten and school in one environment.

The website should present the brand as a unified educational system while clearly showing the two main directions:
- kindergarten
- school

## Main business goal

Generate qualified leads from parents interested in:
- kindergarten
- school
- экскурсия / consultation / admission inquiry

## Secondary goals

- build trust
- explain the value of the educational approach
- show the environment, atmosphere, and quality of the space
- make navigation simple and intuitive
- support future marketing and analytics efforts

## User types

### 1. Parents who already know what they need
Examples:
- “I need a kindergarten”
- “I am looking for a school for my child”

They should quickly find the relevant route and CTA.

### 2. Parents who are still exploring
Examples:
- “What kind of place is this?”
- “How is this different from other options?”
- “Could this work for my child long-term?”

They should be able to understand the full product before choosing a specific route.

## UX principle

Homepage should use **soft routing**, not forced choice.

Meaning:
- users should see the full product clearly
- users may choose a route early if ready
- users should also be able to continue exploring without pressure

## Brand and UX direction

The site should feel:
- premium
- clean
- modern
- warm
- trustworthy
- structured
- calm, not noisy

## What must be preserved from the current version

- overall storytelling logic
- visual direction
- section hierarchy
- clear product presentation
- early but non-aggressive route segmentation
- navigation clarity

## What must be improved

- engineering quality
- maintainability
- scalability
- performance
- SEO
- analytics readiness
- testing readiness
- future integrations
- production deployment quality

## Success criteria

The rebuilt website should:
- look as good as or better than the current version
- preserve the current strong UX logic
- be easier to maintain and extend
- support serious long-term development
- perform well technically
- be safe for future integrations and experiments
